# Voice

% voice-check: skip

Every line of prose the app shows the reader and every line the partner generates follows these rules. They are the owner's standing style rules. Hold the interface copy, the empty states, the button labels, and the partner's output to them.

No em dashes, en dashes, or double hyphens. Use parentheses, commas, colons, or rewrite the sentence.

Full sentences. Do not use sentence fragments as a style. Do not stack two headings with no text between them.

Name what a thing does. Do not use the words "load-bearing" or "register" as jargon.

No AI filler vocabulary: delve, navigate, landscape, multifaceted, indeed, arguably, moreover, "however" as an opener, robust, leverage, comprehensive, nuanced, "key" as an adjective, foster, underscore, seamless, holistic, paradigm, tapestry, vibrant, game-changing.

No throat-clearing openers. Do not start with "It is important to note," "Of course," or "Let me explain."

Do not tell the reader what the app or the partner will not do. Just do the thing. Skip the meta-commentary.

Avoid the cadences that read as machine-written: balanced antitheses, three-part lists built for rhythm, signposted insights ("the deeper point is"), resonant closing lines, and the "it is not X, it is Y" reframe.

When you do not know what a word should point at, leave it open or ask. Do not fill a gap with confident-sounding nouns.

Hebrew and Aramaic appear in Hebrew characters, right to left, with a transliteration in parentheses on first appearance, never transliteration standing alone.

Active voice with a named actor.

Set type large by default. The owner reads on a phone and prefers large text.

Do not put AI or build-tool fingerprints in anything the reader sees or exports. No "generated by" lines.
