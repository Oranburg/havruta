# Project plan: the shape the bridge gives

This plan takes the stance in `docs/VISION.md`, that the app is a bridge to the beit midrash, and lets it organize the whole project. Read it with `docs/VISION.md` (the stance), `docs/ROADMAP.md` (the build phases), and `docs/HOW-DAF-IS-LEARNED.md` (the practice it serves).

## The idea that organizes everything

The app is a bridge to the beit midrash. That does more than set a tone. It gives the project a shape: an arc that carries a person from studying alone, toward studying with help, toward studying with others, toward studying in person. Every page should either deepen the solo study or move the reader one step along that arc. The app should never pretend to be the hall. It should always point toward it.

## The arc

Alone, then with help, then with others, then in person.

- Alone: the daily page and the record. A person keeps the daf even when no one else is there.
- With help: the partner, the commentaries, the lexicon, the read-aloud, the learn page. The page opens.
- With others: study rooms and a found partner. Solo study becomes shared.
- In person: a local shiur, a real chevruta, a hall full of argument. The far side of the bridge.

The Why page draws this arc as a diagram and tells it with images, with the in-person scenes as the aspiration and the solo and remote scenes as the honest present.

## The pages

Each page has a place on the arc, a purpose, and an apparatus (prose, images, diagrams). Status is where it stands today.

- Today (/), alone and with help. The daily core: the page image, the text, the reading, the partner. Status: built.
- The daf, learned (/learn), the soul. Why and how the daf is learned in a beit midrash. Status: built, MDX with Mermaid and images.
- Why this exists (/why), the soul. The bridge stance and the arc, told with the image story. Status: building now.
- Start here (/start), onboarding. How to use the app, how to set up the free partner, why you write first. Status: planned.
- Your journey through Shas (/shas), alone and milestone. The map of the whole Talmud, plus a companion on the cycle and the Siyum HaShas. Status: map built, content companion planned.
- Find your way in (/find), the bridge made concrete. Real ways to find a daf yomi shiur, a chevruta, and a minyan, so the app points a person toward in-person learning. Status: planned, needs current verified resources before it ships.
- Key terms (/terms), with help. The Hebrew and Aramaic vocabulary the study uses, from the running glossary. Status: planned.
- Study together (rooms), with others. Asynchronous then synchronous rooms with the partner present. Status: future, Phase 3, needs a backend and its own plan.
- Your record (/archive), alone. The saved sessions. Status: built.
- Settings and credits, with help. Provider and key, calibration, the read-aloud, the credits. Status: built.

## How the framing changes what already exists

The partner may, sparingly and never as nagging, point a reader toward finding a real chevruta. The learn and why pages are essential rather than decoration, because they carry the soul. The image set tells the arc. Phase 3 rooms are reframed: not only a feature, but the step on the arc from alone to together. The bridge is the reason the rooms matter.

## Build order

1. Publish this plan. Done.
2. Build /why now. Its content and its lead image already exist, and the solo and remote images slot in as they are generated.
3. Build /find next. This is the bridge made concrete, so it must use current, verified resources and real links, never an invented directory. It needs a short research step first.
4. Build /start and /terms.
5. Phase 3 rooms, when its phase comes, with its own plan because it needs a backend.

## What stays fixed on every page

The principles in `docs/CONSTITUTION.md` and `docs/VISION.md` hold everywhere: the partner never invents sacred text, the human acts first, reading is free, the owner's costs are protected, and the app is a bridge and not a replacement.
