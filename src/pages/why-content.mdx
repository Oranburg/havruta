# A bridge to the beit midrash

<LearnImage
  src="learn/hero.jpg"
  alt="A study hall full of pairs learning together"
  caption="A study hall full of pairs learning together."
/>

The best place to learn the daf is a real beit midrash, with a partner across the table and a room full of voices. That is the aspiration this app keeps in view.

Not everyone can be there. Distance, time, health, and where life has put a person keep many of us from the hall. People learn the daf in more than one way now. Some sit together in a study hall. Some meet on a video call across cities and time zones. Some learn alone, in spare minutes, on a phone.

This app is for the ones who cannot get to the hall. It does not replace it, and it should never pretend to. The hope is smaller and truer than that. Studying this way keeps you in the daf, day after day, and it can be a bridge, G-d willing, toward the real thing, an in-person chevruta and a hall full of argument.

## The arc

<Mermaid
  caption="The arc. From studying alone, toward studying with help, toward studying with others, toward studying in person."

Study tends to move along a path. You begin alone with the page. You take on help, a partner who challenges you, the commentaries, the words read aloud. In time you study with others. The far side of the bridge is in person, a local shiur and a real chevruta. The app is built to move you along that path rather than hold you on the near side.

## What that asks of every page

Every page here either deepens your study or points you one step further along the arc. The partner challenges you so the page becomes yours. The learn page shows how the daf is studied where it is studied best. And when you are ready, the app will help you find a shiur and a partner near you, because the point was always to get you to the table.
